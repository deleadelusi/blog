Question 4: What is Wordfish?

Wordfish is a statistical technique and text analysis method used in computational linguistics and natural language processing to identify the underlying dimensions or topics in a collection of texts. It’s a method that is particularly useful for exploring and visualizing the semantic relationships between words in a large corpus of text data.

The main idea behind Wordfish is to represent words as points in a high-dimensional space based on their co-occurrence patterns in the text. By analyzing these patterns, Wordfish can reveal the underlying structure or topics in the text data. This technique is similar in some respects to Latent Semantic Analysis (LSA) and Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA), which are other popular methods for topic modeling and text analysis
